Overview

Wikispeedia is an online game where players navigate from one Wikipedia article to another using only hyperlinks. This project treats the underlying hyperlink structure as a directed graph — each article is a node, each hyperlink a directed edge — and analyzes its structure, community formation, and statistical significance.

We generated four induced subgraphs of 200 randomly sampled nodes, applied three community detection algorithms, and ran a 1,000-iteration Monte Carlo null model test against the full network to confirm that the detected community structure is far stronger than chance.

An average path length of 3.18 confirms small-world properties — most articles are reachable in just a few clicks, which is precisely what makes the game playable. The reciprocity of 22.1% reflects the tendency of related articles to cross-reference each other.

Community Detection

Three algorithms were applied to an undirected version of Subgraph 1. All three found strong community structure; Louvain and Fast Greedy converged on identical partitions, confirming the communities are stable structural features rather than algorithmic artifacts.

Key Findings
01
Hub dominance. United States ranked first across all three centrality measures — in-degree (1,551), betweenness (1,982,737), and eigenvector centrality (1.000). A small set of broad articles serve as unavoidable waypoints regardless of navigation route.
02
Heavy-tailed degree distribution. The in-degree distribution follows a power-law pattern on a log-log plot: a handful of articles absorb the vast majority of links while most have very few — characteristic of real-world information networks.
03
Topically coherent communities. Science articles clustered with science, geography with geography. Reordering the adjacency matrix by community membership revealed clear block-diagonal structure, visually confirming the communities reflect real semantic groupings.
04
Statistically significant structure. The Monte Carlo null model — 1,000 Erdős–Rényi random graphs with matched node and edge counts — produced a null distribution with maximum modularity well below the observed Q = 0.372. The result: p = 0. Community structure is not random.
